What Are the Benefits of A Reusable Air Filter?

A reusable air filter is an essential component of a central air conditioning system. It traps and removes dust, dirt, pollen and other contaminants from the air before it reaches your home’s indoor environment. A reusable filter is essential when it comes to maintaining your HVAC system and reducing operational costs. These filters are usually washable and reusable, so they don’t add to the cost of purchasing new filters. While a new filter can cost anywhere between $30 and $50, reusable filters cost between $5 and $10. However, the benefits of a reusable filter are worth the cost. These filters are cheaper than new ones, can be used for years and will help clean indoor air of contaminants such as dust, pet dander, viruses and bacteria.

Reduce VOC and Carbon Dioxide Emissions

Reusable filters trap and remove contaminants, such as dust, dirt, pollen and viruses. These contaminants are released into the indoor air as VOCs and carbon dioxide. VOCs are a type of organic compound, which can cause eye, nose and throat irritation. Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas that traps heat in the atmosphere. Therefore, the fewer contaminants in the air, the less VOCs and carbon dioxide released into the air. These contaminants can cause poor indoor air quality, which can be detrimental to your health. Reusable filters will trap these contaminants and prevent them from being released back into the air.

Better Air Quality

Reusable filters trap and remove contaminants from the air. These contaminants can cause poor indoor air quality, which can be detrimental to your health. For example, dust mites, bacteria, viruses and pollens can cause allergic reactions, headaches and other health issues. By trapping and removing these contaminants from the air, reusable filters will improve indoor air quality. This will also help reduce the risk of contracting diseases.

Help Protect Against Allergens and Dust Mites

Dust mites, pet dander and viruses are common contaminants in indoor air. Dust mites, which are microscopic, feed on human skin flakes. They are found in almost every home and cause allergic reactions in many people. Reusable filters trap and remove these contaminants, helping to protect against allergies and respiratory issues. Pet dander, which is a type of hair found in cats and dogs, can cause allergies in some people. Reusable filters trap and remove pet dander, preventing it from being released back into the air. Viruses, such as the flu virus, can be released into the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. Reusable filters trap and remove these viruses, preventing them from being released back into the air.

Lower Operating Costs

Reusable filters trap and remove dust, dirt, pollen and other contaminants from the air. These contaminants cause wear and tear on the HVAC system, resulting in higher operating costs. Reusable filters will extend the life of your HVAC system and reduce the amount of money you spend on repairs. They will also trap and remove contaminants from the air, preventing these contaminants from damaging your HVAC system. Reusable filters cost less than new filters, but they are essential to extending the life of your HVAC system and reducing operational costs.

Conclusion

Reusable filters trap and remove dust, dirt, pollen and other contaminants from the air, helping to improve indoor air quality and protect against allergies and respiratory issues. They are essential to extending the life of your HVAC system and reducing operational costs. Reusable filters cost less than new filters, but they are essential to extending the life of your HVAC system and reducing operational costs.

Frequently Asked Question

How frequently do I have to change our air filter?
Make sure you change your air filter every 3 months or 90 days if you live in the suburbs. If you seldom visit your house or if you live alone and oftentimes spend more hours at work, then you can change your air filter every 6 – 12 months. However, if you have a pet, you ought to change it every 2 months. If you have more than one pet or if anyone at home has allergies, make sure to clean it every 20-45 days.
How can a dirty filter affect my AC?
A dirty filter can create damage to your AC. Dust accumulation or too much build-up can cause your AC to work improperly. It would also entail an increase in your electricity bills since it will pressure the unit to work hard to compensate for the desired output. Soon your unit will experience a failure in its system, making you spend more in the process.

What will happen if my AC filter is dirty?
Technically, dust will build up in your AC Unit. Soon it will find its way circulating inside your room giving you allergies and respiratory problems. It will also restrict the flow of cold air which will cause a buildup in your unit. In time, the build-up will cause your unit to ice up hampering its performance.
